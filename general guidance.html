General Guidance

Climbing is not only an opportunity for you to face your own problems: limits, endurance, endurance... but also a challenge to nature and unexpected factors. Therefore, it is extremely important to prepare carefully for a long or short climbing trip, at home or abroad.

To conquer a peak, in addition to essential items such as food and water, do not forget to exercise and prepare yourself for a beautiful soul.

What to prepare for a self-sufficient climbing trip?

Exploring and equally adventurous journeys always give us many unforgettable experiences in life. If you are a genuine backpacker, surely you cannot ignore the peak conquests such as waterfalls, slopes or dangerous cliffs.

Climbing a mountain can take a day, or several days depending on the altitude. Traveling in the mountain road with the harsh environment forces you to carefully prepare the necessary luggage. Visitors should equip themselves with the following items:

  • 1
    Find out information

    Finding out information about climbing places is a top thing, but sometimes it is easily overlooked for many reasons: maybe you are too subjective or easily "fooled" by many beautiful pictures of the mountain. mountains.Information about the mountain is essential for you to plan your physical training and equipment before the trip. The height of the mountain, the temperature difference between day and night, the seasonal weather will determine how much food you will bring, thin or thick clothes.

  • 2
    Exercise

    You must have health to go climbing, for what? In order not to burden the rest of the group when they have to go slow to wait for you alone, or have to carry your backpack or even carry you when you are exhausted. Healthy so as not to spend money on renting horses or chartering planes to take you down the mountain. And above all, you have to be strong to have the strength to take pictures, see the mountains, enjoy the trip that you have been waiting for and cost money to get.

  • 3
    Prepare all the equipment

    As mentioned, climbing is a journey in which we come face to face with ourselves and nature. Every item we carry is a tool to survive, protect, and help us.Therefore, choose really strong backpacks, with support straps on the chest and abdomen to help evenly distribute the force to the body. And don't forget, the backpack covers help you avoid rain and dirt when traveling long distances.The top criterion when choosing clothes is lightweight because anything can become a burden for you on the mountain road.

  • 4
    MONITOR THE WEATHER REGULARLY

    For a climbing trip, the weather is the most direct and important factor. Therefore, before going on the road, visitors need to monitor the weather forecast regularly. At the same time, along the way, you must also observe the surrounding weather changes. This will help visitors navigate the path and make a reasonable decision to stop or continue.

  • 5
    Go in groups

    This is something that those who intend to climb the mountain need to pay special attention to. Climbing is a long journey full of difficulties and challenges. Whether you are a seasoned traveler or a beginner, climbing is not allowed alone. Because, following and strictly following the rules of the organization or an experienced porter will help visitors have a safer and more effective journey.

  • 6
    Prepare for life skills

    Life skills such as going to the forest, working in groups, seeking support, etc. are essential for mountain climbers. Knowledge of breathing control when climbing, regulating body temperature when there is a change in altitude, or knowing how to give first aid to some injuries or encounters such as scratches on limbs, cramps, snake bites, etc. These are things that every climber needs to master. Whether you are traveling with a group or alone, life skills always help you to have the best way to handle and overcome difficulties in case of need.

The best climbing technique and posture (How to?)

Experienced climbers don't use full strength to climb walls, but use their technique using moves designed to help them deal with specific problems. If you want to become a better climber, the best climbing techniques and postures in this article will help you.

Technical innovation related to the principles of motion and balance. Then you can focus on following your body movements on the ledge.

1. Mountain climbing techniques

The importance of climbing technique is essential. As you focus on technique, your body's movements automatically snap into place and you find yourself easily gripping routes that were once too difficult.

2.Mountain climbing pose

Having knowledge of climbing posture helps you solve problems and tackle more difficult routes. Each of these moves uses the principles mentioned in the technique section, above.

This section covers some key concepts:

  • Step back is the opposite of a normal step. Instead of stepping on one toe with your big toe and hip perpendicular to the wall, you rotate your hip to the side and step with the outside edge (side of the little toe) of the shoe.
  • Lowering the knee is a bigger step backwards. It works best when you have a standing position near hip level. Step on it with your toes; then roll your knee inward until the outside of your shoe rests on the sole and your knee is actually pointing down.
  • Hanging is a balancing pose that uses one limb to shift your weight. The goal is to stay away from the rock.

3.Tips for climbing for beginners

Here are some suggestions:

  • Short roads suitable for ability
  • Only bring what is absolutely necessary
  • Choosing the right shoes and socks is very important
  • Learn how to use survival items
  • Allocate energy for the whole journey
