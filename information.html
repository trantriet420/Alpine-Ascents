Trekking (climbing) is often understood as an adventure sport, conquering the highest peaks, difficult and new, requiring specific skills, for those who are professional in adventure. But in another direction, more popular, chosen by some people today, trekking is a type of adventure mountain tourism to explore nature, conquer the peaks safely, at a moderate level, suitability, is allowed.

The journey to conquer a peak almost entirely using one's own strength, walking, being able to wade, climb, swing, cling to some difficult terrain always brings intense emotions and feelings of a person. Go explore, challenge yourself, overcome your own limits, appreciate and protect nature.

Climbing (Trekking) - Conquer, explore, overcome your limits

The climb is hard but also very unlucky. If you are lucky, in addition to the inherent beauty of the mountains, you will also encounter wonderful moments of nature. The weather resonates with the mountains and forests, the terrain creates the most perfect, wonderful scenery.

The most important thing on a trekking journey is discovering your own emotions, feelings, abilities and limitations. A journey of two or three days until looking back, you will learn a lot from listening to people, listening to yourself, knowing where you are, what was best for you, and then those things will govern decisions. another in your life, or you learn to deal with your problems as you get to know yourself better.

Lai Chau - Mountain climbing paradise

Talking about the potential to develop a specific type of mountaineering tourism product, Lai Chau is fortunate to possess outstanding strengths. This northwestern border strip has six of the ten highest peaks in Vietnam. Overcoming impressive trekking journeys, mountaineers will have the opportunity to enjoy stunning natural wonders. From "cloud hunting paradise" Ky Quan San (Bach Moc Luong Tu - 3,046m) to the "three-hour slope" up to Po Ma Lung (2,967m).

From admiring the "most beautiful mountain peak" Pu Ta Leng (3,049m) to touching milestone 42 on the journey to discover Pu Si Lung (3,083m) burning with azalea colors. From exploring Co Trau-Ta Lien Son (2,996m) to conquering the beautiful primeval forest at the top of Khang Su Van (3,012m). Exhausted after hours of crossing streams and forests, visitors will be immersed in the magical natural landscape, cool off under the white waterfalls, immersed in the paradise blooming azaleas.

A rough gem waiting to be honed-Lai Chau

According to the assessment of many adventure tourism experts, the mountain peaks in Lai Chau mostly keep the pristine status of old forests and the discovery route with high difficulty, defying all limits of nature. perseverance and courage of visitors. Along with that, the residents living around the mountains are still indigenous ethnic villages, preserving their own inherent cultural identity. Those are the necessary and sufficient factors to help this type of business take off, if there is a methodical brand formation and positioning strategy.
