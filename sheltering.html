Sheltering

In the natural environment, in addition to food, how to find shelter in the forest (mountain) is also a very important part. Imagine when the night covers everything around, you can't go around in the dark or lie down in the middle of the forest, right?

Shelter can both help you avoid sudden temperature drops in the evening, unexpected rainstorms or even the attack of predators, reptiles as well as insects. Above all, finding a good shelter will also help you have an optimistic and much stronger spirit in the cold night of mother nature.

This article will help you find shelter as quickly as possible depending on the conditions and natural environment around at that time.

1. Summary of How to Find Shelter in the Forest (mountain)

 First of all, you need to keep a few factors in mind before you seek shelter as follows:
  * Find a place where the ground is flat and well ventilated.
  * Find the land far away and higher than rivers and streams, avoid waterlogging when it rains
  * Stay away from lowland areas, shallow stream beds, if there is a flood, you can't run in time
  * Avoid places with bushes and tall grass because of snakes
  * Avoid the direction of the wind

2.  Sleeping in the tree

This is a place where you can sleep safely off the ground, which is also very convenient if you are in an area that often rains, swamps, etc.

Look around the area you stand and look for large, branched trees. If a tree is big enough and has many branches, it will give you an ideal resting place. You can pick up more tree trunks that are strong enough to cross, to increase the area enough to rest on your back.

If conditions permit, you can gather many branches and leaves to make a film around it to prevent the temperature from dropping at night. Sleeping in a tree can help you avoid hypothermia from the ground, or worries from insects, snakes or nocturnal predators very effectively.

Note: To prevent lightning strikes during thunderstorms, you should avoid large, open areas. At the same time, it should not be located too high.

Similarly, you can still flexibly choose big stumps, or low trunks, as long as there are enough 4 corners to use as the main pillar, and then also find a sturdy enough horizontal bar to make a bed, you can collect fresh leaves, Fresh grass has been heated through the fire to make the mattress warm again.

3.  Sleep in a makeshift tent

If it is not possible to sleep off the ground, we can set up a temporary tent, this form is simpler but can still give you a relatively safe shelter.

Look for large fallen logs that can block the wind well. Use this horizontal broken trunk as a permanent stand, then you can take other branches to make a frame and cover the outside with trees.

If there are small trunks, you can cut down the middle and fold the tree down to create a shelter for yourself. Even if you can't cut it, you can still grab the rope and pull it down. With this temporary tent you can still light a fire to warm up inside, provided of course you don't make the tent too tight.

In the same way, you can take advantage of large stone blocks, large tree stumps ... to make pillars, as long as we create an extra layer of apricot shell above and wrap around to protect when sleeping. These are all types of makeshift tents that are easy to make with simple materials such as wood, stone, leaves, etc. to help you get a good shelter in the wild natural environment.

4. Sleeping in a cave

For most people, caves are great places to hide. But you also need to keep a few points in mind when choosing a cave as a shelter.

How to search caves

Bats often gather in caves, you can observe and follow the flying direction of bats. Brown crickets are also animals that live near caves and ravines

Check out karst areas, crevices with wind or water that often have access to caves

The rocky areas along the coast are prone to caves, but be careful with the tides by observing traces of wet soil, moss, etc.

Tips for sleeping in a cave

Bats often gather in groups deep in caves, you can stay outside and still not have too much trouble with them

Although most small mammals such as mice, skunks, raccoons... often live in caves, but should also be wary and watch out for larger predators.

Snakes, scorpions, and spiders love this place, so make sure you keep an eye on them and chase them away before resting here.

Absolutely do not set fire in the cave, if the cave is small, it is easy to suffocate you because of the loss of oxygen.

Besides, remember the bats that used to live in caves? Bat droppings are extremely explosive near a fire, so it's best to burn them at the entrance of the cave.

Do not be curious to go too deep into the cave, because the deeper you go, the more oxygen you will lose, you may faint before you realize the danger

Deep inside the cave can also have deep pools, swamps, slippery slopes and easy to get lost

Pay attention to the sound of water, because flooding in the cave can happen very quickly for many reasons such as residual water due to heavy rains, or this cave is a low-lying area that receives water when it rains... If you see a sudden increase in the amount of water or hear the sound of rushing water, quickly get out of here
